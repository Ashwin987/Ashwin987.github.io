Monitoring Injury Risk & Performance: Chelsea FC Vizathon

Tech: Python, Power BI, Random Forest, Time-Series Analysis

Addressed the challenge of balancing match demands, training intensity, and recovery for Chelsea FC. I developed the Load Balance Efficiency Score (LBES), a composite metric scaled 0-100 that unifies disparate data sources (GPS, wellness, and physical testing) to quantify player readiness.

The project implemented a Random Forest Classifier to predict injury risk sessions with 100% precision for high-risk cases. I also designed an interactive Power BI dashboard for performance staff to visualize trends, detecting "red zone" spikes in workload (>20% variance) and estimating recovery timelines based on historical data.

Optimizing FPL Scoring for Defensive Midfielders

Tech: Python, Web Scraping, FBref Data

Defensive midfielders are notoriously undervalued in Fantasy Premier League (FPL) because the scoring system favors goal contributions over defensive work. I built a web scraper to extract player data from the FPL archive and match reports from FBref for the 2021-22 season to analyze this disparity.

To solve this imbalance, I proposed and tested a new scoring metric: awarding 1 point for every 3 ball recoveries (interceptions + tackles won). This adjustment would close the point gap between defensive midfielders and attacking players, making the game more strategically diverse without overcomplicating the rules.

EPL Team Performance Analysis Using Modeling & Visualization

Tech: R (sqldf, glm), Poisson Regression, K-Means Clustering, Tableau

Investigated the efficiency of English Premier League clubs by analyzing the relationship between financial resources and on-field success. I collected data from FBref and Transfermarkt, using R's sqldf package to merge seasonal performance metrics with squad characteristics.

I applied K-Means Clustering to identify "efficient buyers" (like Brighton & Brentford) and implemented Poisson Regression models to predict win totals. The analysis revealed that while relative spending is a significant predictor, older squads and high player turnover rates show a statistically significant negative impact on performance.
